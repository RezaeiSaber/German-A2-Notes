## 1. Reflexive Verbs (Reflexive Verben) [01:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wJzKKNM51nM&t=91s)

Reflexive verbs are verbs where the action reflects back to the subject. In German, these verbs are accompanied by a reflexive pronoun.

### Reflexive Pronouns Table
Most reflexive pronouns are identical to the accusative personal pronouns, except for the third person (singular and plural) and the formal "Sie".

| Subject | Reflexive Pronoun | Subject | Reflexive Pronoun |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ich** | **mich** | **wir** | **uns** |
| **du** | **dich** | **ihr** | **euch** |
| **er/sie/es** | **sich** | **sie/Sie** | **sich** |

### Sentence Structure
In a standard sentence, the reflexive pronoun usually occupies the third position.
* **Formula:** Subject (Pos. 1) + Conjugated Verb (Pos. 2) + Reflexive Pronoun (Pos. 3).

### Examples:
* **sich bedanken (to thank/express gratitude):**
    * *Ich bedanke mich.* (I thank [myself].)
    * *Du bedankst dich.* (You thank [yourself].)
    * *Wir bedanken uns.* (We thank [ourselves].)

## 4. Formal Letter Writing (Formales Schreiben) [27:00](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wJzKKNM51nM&t=1620s)

When writing formal emails or letters in German, specific salutations and closings are required.

### Salutations (Anrede):
* **To a woman:** *Sehr geehrte Frau [Nachname],* (Very esteemed Ms. [Last Name])
* **To a man:** *Sehr geehrter Herr [Nachname],* (Very esteemed Mr. [Last Name])
* **To an unknown person/organization:** *Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,* (Dear Sir or Madam)

### Important Rules:
1. Always use a comma after the salutation.
2. The first word of the letter body starts with a **small letter** (unless it's a noun).

### Closing (Gru√üformel):
* *Mit freundlichen Gr√º√üen* (With friendly greetings/Yours sincerely)
* Note: No comma is used after the closing in German before signing your name.

## 1. Dative Endings for "dies-", "welch-", and others [00:31](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyK7E84R6U8&t=31s)

Just like the definite articles (*der, die, das*), words like **dies-** (this), **welch-** (which), **and-** (other), and **erst-** (first) must take specific endings based on the gender and case of the noun they describe.

### Dative Endings Table
| Gender | Article (Dative) | "dies-" (this) | "welch-" (which) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Masculine** | dem | **diesem** | **welchem** |
| **Neuter** | dem | **diesem** | **welchem** |
| **Feminine** | der | **dieser** | **welcher** |
| **Plural** | den (+n) | **diesen** (+n) | **welchen** (+n) |

### Key Grammar Rules:
* **Prepositions triggering Dative:** Words like *nach* (after), *vor* (before), and *in* (when describing a static location/no movement) always require the dative case.
* **Plural Dative N-rule:** In the dative plural, the noun itself usually gets an extra **-n** at the end (e.g., *L√§nder* becomes *L√§ndern*). [06:14](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyK7E84R6U8&t=374s)

### Examples:

## 2. Advanced Reflexive Verbs: Accusative vs. Dative [23:53](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyK7E84R6U8&t=1433s)

While most reflexive pronouns are in the accusative, some verbs use the dative reflexive pronoun. This often happens when there is already another direct object (accusative) in the sentence.

### Reflexive Pronouns Comparison:
| Subject | Accusative | Dative |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ich** | mich | **mir** |
| **du** | dich | **dir** |
| **er/sie/es** | sich | sich |
| **wir** | uns | uns |
| **ihr** | euch | euch |
| **sie/Sie** | sich | sich |

### Verbs with Meaning Changes:
* **sich vorstellen (Accusative):** To introduce oneself.
    * *Ich stelle mich vor.* (I introduce myself.) [25:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyK7E84R6U8&t=1533s)
* **sich vorstellen (Dative):** To imagine.
    * *Ich stelle mir vor.* (I imagine [to myself].) [25:55](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyK7E84R6U8&t=1555s)

### The "Body Part/Clothing" Rule:
If you name a specific body part or item of clothing being acted upon, the reflexive pronoun becomes **Dative**.
* *Ich ziehe **mich** an.* (I get dressed - Accusative)
* *Ich ziehe **mir** die Jacke an.* (I put on the jacket - Dative) [27:35](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyK7E84R6U8&t=1655s)
* *Ich habe **mich** verletzt.* (I injured myself - Accusative)
* *Ich habe **mir** den Fu√ü verletzt.* (I injured my foot - Dative) [28:27](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyK7E84R6U8&t=1707s)
