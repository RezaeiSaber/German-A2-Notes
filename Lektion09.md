## 2. Deep Dive: Accusative and Dative Endings [02:11](https://youtube.com/watch?v=-7VVaDavb1g&t=131s)

### Accusative Case [02:11](https://youtube.com/watch?v=-7VVaDavb1g&t=131s)
Only the **Masculine** gender changes compared to the Nominative case.
* **Masculine:** "ein" becomes "einen", and the adjective ending becomes **-en**.
    * Example: **Ich habe einen gro√üen Mann gesehen.** (I saw a tall man.) [02:47](https://youtube.com/watch?v=-7VVaDavb1g&t=167s)
* **Neuter/Feminine/Plural:** Endings remain the same as in the Nominative case.

### Dative Case [03:41](https://youtube.com/watch?v=-7VVaDavb1g&t=221s)

* **Masculine/Neuter:** "einem" + adjective ending **-en**.
    * Example: **Ich danke einem gro√üen Mann.** (I thank a tall man.) [04:07](https://youtube.com/watch?v=-7VVaDavb1g&t=247s)

    * Example: **Ich schenke meiner sch√∂nen Frau etwas.** (I give my beautiful wife something.) [04:45](https://youtube.com/watch?v=-7VVaDavb1g&t=285s)
* **Plural:** "meinen" + adjective ending **-en**.
