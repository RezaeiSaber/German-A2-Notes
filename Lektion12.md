## Pronominal Adverbs: Questions and Pronouns [00:02:14](https://youtu.be/A5GmT3ncqG8&t=134s)

In German, when a verb has a fixed preposition (e.g., *warten auf*, *tr√§umen von*), we create questions and pronouns specifically for **things** (not people) using the structures **Wo(r)-** and **Da(r)-**.

### 1. Questions with "Wo(r) + Preposition" [00:03:14](https://youtu.be/A5GmT3ncqG8&t=194s)
To ask "What ... about?" or "For what?", we combine **wo** + the preposition.
* **Rule:** If the preposition starts with a vowel (a, e, i, o, u), add an "**r**" in between.

* **tr√§umen von (to dream of):**

  * **Worauf wartet er?** (What is he waiting for?) [00:06:12](https://youtube.com/watch?v=A5GmT3ncqG8&t=372s)
* **denken an (to think of):**
  * `wo` + `r` + `an` = **Woran** [00:09:18](https://youtube.com/watch?v=A5GmT3ncqG8&t=558s)

## Prepositions of Origin: aus vs. von [00:01:26](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=86s)

Both **aus** and **von** indicate "from," but they are used in different contexts. Both require the **Dative** case.

### 1. Using "von" (+ Dative) [00:02:21](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=141s)
Use **von** when coming from:
* **A person:** *Ich komme vom Arzt.* (I am coming from the doctor.) [00:03:00](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=180s)
* **An activity:** *Ich komme vom Training.* (I am coming from training.) [00:03:31](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=211s)
* **A general point/location (without entering):** *Ich komme vom Bahnhof.* (I am coming from the train station.) [00:05:25](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=325s)

### 2. Using "aus" (+ Dative) [00:04:06](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=246s)
Use **aus** when coming from:
* **Inside a space/building:** *Ich komme aus dem Haus.* (I am coming out of the house.) [00:04:29](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=269s)
* **A city/country:** *Ich komme aus dem Iran.* (I come from Iran.)

### 3. "bis zu" (until/up to) [00:06:45](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=405s)
Used to indicate a destination limit.
* **Der Bus f√§hrt bis zum Stadion.** (The bus goes as far as the stadium.) [00:06:58](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=418s)
* **Wir laufen bis zur Br√ºcke.** (We run as far as the bridge.) [00:07:05](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=425s)

## Local Prepositions: in, auf, an [00:22:14](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=1334s)

Choosing the right preposition depends on the nature of the location and whether there is movement (**Akkusativ**) or position (**Dativ**).

### 1. Preposition Summaries [00:23:55](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=1435s)
* **auf + das = aufs**
* **an + dem = am**
* **an + das = ans**
* **in + dem = im**
* **in + das = ins**

### 2. "auf" (Open Spaces & Public Places) [00:24:29](https://youtube.com/watch?v=68sDGz3XO2E&t=1469s)
