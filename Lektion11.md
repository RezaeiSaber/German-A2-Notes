## Indirect Questions (Indirekte Fragen) [00:01:07](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=67s)

Indirect questions are more polite than direct questions. They are introduced by a main clause (e.g., "Do you know...", "Can you tell me...").

### Grammar Structure: Nebensatz
Indirect questions function as subordinate clauses (**Nebensatz**). This means the conjugated verb moves to the very end of the sentence.

* **Direct:** Wie sp√§t ist es? (What time is it?)
* **Indirect:** Wei√üt du, wie sp√§t es **ist**? (Do you know what time it is?) [00:02:47](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=167s)

* **Direct:** Wie kann man das kochen? (How can one cook that?)
* **Indirect:** Kannst du mir sagen, wie man das kochen **kann**? (Can you tell me how one can cook that?) [00:03:35](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=215s)

* **Direct:** Wer hat das Handy gestohlen? (Who stole the mobile phone?)
* **Indirect:** Hast du eine Idee, wer das Handy gestohlen **hat**? (Do you have an idea who stole the mobile phone?) [00:04:28](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=268s)

* **Direct:** Welches Buch kann ich ihm empfehlen? (Which book can I recommend to him?)
* **Indirect:** Er fragte mich, welches Buch ich ihm empfehlen **kann**. (He asked me which book I can recommend to him.) [00:05:15](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=315s)

## Subordinate Clauses with "ob" (whether/if) [00:05:20](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=320s)

The conjunction **ob** is used for "yes/no" questions that are made indirect. It translates to "whether" or "if" (in the sense of "if or not").

### Rules for "ob":
1. It is a **Nebensatz** conjunction (verb goes to the end).
2. It cannot start a standalone sentence; it must follow a main clause.

* **Example 1:** Ich bin mir nicht sicher, **ob** er heute kommt. (I am not sure whether he is coming today.) [00:06:19](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=379s)
* **Example 2:** Wei√üt du, **ob** der Supermarkt noch offen ist? (Do you know if the supermarket is still open?) [00:06:59](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=419s)
* **Example 3:** Gib mir bitte Bescheid, **ob** du heute mitkommst. (Please let me know if you are coming along today.) [00:07:35](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=455s)
* **Example 4:** Ich frage mich, **ob** ich genug Geld dabei habe. (I wonder if I have enough money with me.) [00:08:14](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=494s)

## Passive Voice: The Agent (von vs. durch) [00:12:27](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=747s)

In a passive sentence, if you want to mention who or what performed the action, you use **von** or **durch**.

### 1. "von" + Dative [00:13:16](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=796s)
Used for **living beings** (humans, animals) or **conscious institutions** (the government, the school board).
* **Example:** Der Pr√§sident wird **von** der Abstimmung gew√§hlt. (The president is elected by the vote [human action].) [00:15:57](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=957s)
* **Example:** Das Gesetz wurde **von** der Regierung beschlossen. (The law was decided by the government.) [00:17:10](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=1030s)

### 2. "durch" + Accusative [00:14:13](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=853s)
Used for **non-living agents**, instruments, or natural forces (wind, fire, etc.).
* **Example:** Das Fenster ist **durch** den Wind zerbrochen. (The window is broken by the wind.) [00:18:53](https://youtube.com/watch?v=kv108M42Sj8&t=1133s)
