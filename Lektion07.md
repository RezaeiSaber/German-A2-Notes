## 1. üÜöÔ∏è Prepositions: mit vs. ohne [01:00](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=60s)

* **ohne (without):** Always triggers the **Akkusativ** case.
* **mit (with):** Always triggers the **Dativ** case.

* **Ich bin mit meinem Handy dort gegangen.** (I went there with my mobile phone.) [02:15](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=135s)
    * *Analysis: In Dativ, "das" becomes "dem", so the possessive ending is "-em" (meinem).*
* **Er ist mit seinem Regenschirm spazieren gegangen.** (He went for a walk with his umbrella.) [02:46](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=166s)

## 2. Temporal Connectors: als vs. wenn [08:05](https://youtube.com/watch?v=xjRYFZoax7o&t=485s)

Both mean "when," but they are used in different contexts. Both are subordinate connectors (verb goes to the end).

| :--- | :--- | :--- |
