## 1. Prepositions: mit vs. ohne [01:00](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=60s)

A fundamental distinction in German is between "with" and "without," which trigger different cases.

### The Rule
* **ohne (without):** Always triggers the **Akkusativ** case.
* **mit (with):** Always triggers the **Dativ** case.

### Examples in Use
* **Ich bin ohne mein Handy dort gegangen.** (I went there without my mobile phone.) [02:03](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=123s)
    * *Analysis: "Handy" is "das". In Akkusativ, "das" remains "das", so "mein" remains "mein".*
* **Ich bin mit meinem Handy dort gegangen.** (I went there with my mobile phone.) [02:15](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=135s)
    * *Analysis: In Dativ, "das" becomes "dem", so the possessive ending is "-em" (meinem).*
* **Er ist mit seinem Regenschirm spazieren gegangen.** (He went for a walk with his umbrella.) [02:46](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=166s)
    * *Analysis: "Regenschirm" is "der". In Dativ, "der" becomes "dem", hence "seinem".*
* **Warum seid ihr ohne mich essen gegangen?** (Why did you go eat without me?) [03:22](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=202s)
    * *Analysis: "mich" is the Akkusativ form of "ich".*
* **Sanaz will mit ihrem Mann nach Deutschland gehen.** (Sanaz wants to go to Germany with her husband.) [03:45](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=225s)
* **Ohne das Auto k√∂nnen wir nicht p√ºnktlich ankommen.** (Without the car, we cannot arrive on time.) [04:15](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=255s)

## 2. Subordinate Clauses with "dass" [06:50](https://youtube.com/watch?v=h1Tam1GXTMw&t=410s)

## 2. Temporal Connectors: als vs. wenn [08:05](https://youtube.com/watch?v=xjRYFZoax7o&t=485s)

Both mean "when," but they are used in different contexts. Both are subordinate connectors (verb goes to the end).

| Connector | Usage | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
