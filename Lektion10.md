## Word Order with Two Objects (Dative & Accusative) [00:24:49](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lceB6DwZXKM&t=1489s)

When a sentence has both a Dative object (usually a person) and an Accusative object (usually a thing), the order depends on whether they are nouns or pronouns.

### The Order Rules:
1.  **Both are Nouns:** Dative comes before Accusative.
    * *Ich gebe **meinem Vater** (Dat) **das Handy** (Akk).*
2.  **One is a Pronoun:** The pronoun always comes first.
    * *Ich gebe **ihm** (Pronoun) **das Handy** (Noun).*
    * *Ich gebe **es** (Pronoun) **meinem Vater** (Noun).*
3.  **Both are Pronouns:** Accusative comes before Dative.
    * *Ich gebe **es** (Akk) **ihm** (Dat).* [00:27:51](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lceB6DwZXKM&t=1671s)

### Further Examples with *zeigen* (to show):
